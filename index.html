FAQ

Common questions from travelers

Is this a medical diagnosis?

No. We are licensed nurses, not doctors. We do not diagnose illnesses, provide treatment, or prescribe medicine. We help you understand your symptoms in general terms, decide where to seek care, and prepare for your hospital visit.

What languages do you support?

English, Spanish, and easy Japanese (やさしい日本語). Spanish is our specialty — our nurses consult directly in Spanish, without machine translation.

Can you find a hospital that accepts my travel insurance?

We help you check. Tell us your insurance company, and we will look for hospitals with cashless service or explain how to pay first and claim reimbursement later. Please have your policy documents ready.

How much does a hospital visit cost in Japan?

Without insurance, a typical clinic visit costs about ¥5,000–¥15,000; emergency or hospital care costs more. We give you a rough estimate before you go, so there are no surprises.

What are your hours? What about the middle of the night?

We answer from 8:00 to 22:00 JST every day. Outside these hours, for anything urgent, call 119 (ambulance) or visit an emergency hospital. The Japan Visitor Hotline (050-3816-2787) also operates 24/7.

Can you interpret for me at the hospital?

We do not send staff to hospitals, but we prepare a written symptom summary in Japanese that you can show to the doctor, and we can suggest phone-interpretation options that many hospitals use.

How do I pay?

Credit card or PayPal, after your consultation. We send you a secure payment link by LINE, WhatsApp, or email.

Important notice
  • This service is provided by licensed nurses and is not a medical institution. We do not perform diagnosis, treatment, or prescription of medicine.
  • Our guidance is general information to support your decisions. It does not replace examination by a doctor.
  • In case of severe symptoms (chest pain, difficulty breathing, heavy bleeding, loss of consciousness, stroke signs), call 119 immediately.
